John Dalton believed that all matter was composed of tiny, indivisible pieces of matter called atoms.

J.J Thomson - 1904

Having discovered the electron, J.J Thomson came up with the "plum pudding" model of the atom, which included negatively charged electrons mixed in a positive mass.

Robert Millikan - 1909

Through his oil drop experiment, Robert Millikan determined the charge of the electron.

Ernest Rutherford - 1911

Following his gold foil experiment, Rutherford proposed an atomic model where the majority of the mass resides in a positive nucleus, while electrons orbit around it.

Neils Bohr - 1915

Improving on Rutherfords planetary model, Bohr proposed that electrons orbit around the nucleus in discrete orbital paths corresponding to their energy level.

Louis de Broglie - 1924

In response to the issues present in past models, Lousis de Broglie proposed a model where the electrons were particles that exhibited wave properties.
