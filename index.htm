Bill's Atomic Theory Timeline!

John Dalton - 1803

John Dalton believed that all matter was composed of tiny, indivisible pieces of matter called atoms. He also proposed that compounds are mixtures of different types of atoms.

J.J Thomson - 1897

Having discovered the electron using a cathode ray tube, J.J Thomson came up with the "plum pudding" model of the atom, which included negatively charged electrons mixed in a positive mass.

Max Planck - 1900

Proposed that energy can only be abosrbed and emitted by particles in discrete units (quanta) and thus came up with Planck's Constant.

Albert Einstein - 1905

Building on Planck's theory, Einstein theorized that light was made of quanta (photons) instead of a wave. This helped explain the photoelectric effect and provided evidence for the concept of wave-particle duality.

Robert Millikan - 1909

Through his oil drop experiment, Robert Millikan determined the charge of the electron.

Ernest Rutherford - 1911

Following his gold foil experiment, Rutherford proposed an atomic model where the majority of the mass resides in a positive nucleus, while negative electrons orbit around it.

Neils Bohr - 1915

Improving on Rutherfords planetary model, Bohr proposed that electrons orbit around the nucleus in discrete orbital paths corresponding to their energy level.

In response to the issues present in past models, Lousis de Broglie proposed a model where the electrons were particles that exhibited wave properties.
